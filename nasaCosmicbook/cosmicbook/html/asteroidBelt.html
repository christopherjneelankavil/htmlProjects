About Asteroid Belt

The asteroid belt is a region of space between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter where most of the asteroids in our Solar System are found orbiting the Sun. It is roughly 2 to 4 times the Earth-Sun distance, and spans a region about 140 million miles across. The asteroid belt contains millions of asteroids, which are small, rocky bodies that range in size from a few feet to hundreds of miles across. The largest asteroid in the belt is Ceres, which is about 587 miles (945 km) in diameter. Ceres is so large that it is classified as a dwarf planet. The asteroid belt formed in the region between Mars and Jupiter, where the centrifugal force was too strong for material to coalesce into a planet. Instead, the material remained as small, rocky bodies. The asteroid belt is a dynamic region of space. The asteroids are constantly colliding with each other, and some of them are ejected from the belt entirely. Some asteroids have also been captured by Jupiter's gravity and become Trojan asteroids, which orbit the Sun in the same or similar orbits as Jupiter.

Ceres

Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt and is classified as a dwarf planet. It is about 587 miles (945 km) in diameter and has a complex surface with craters, mountains, and valleys.

Vesta

Vesta is the second largest asteroid in the belt and is about 326 miles (525 km) in diameter. It is known for its bright surface and its large crater at the south pole, which is thought to have been formed by a giant impact.

Pallas

Pallas is the third largest asteroid in the belt and is about 211 miles (340 km) in diameter. It is known for its irregular shape and its long rotation period, which takes about 7.8 hours.

Hygiea

Hygiea is the fourth largest asteroid in the belt and is about 288 miles (463 km) in diameter. It is known for its bright surface and its large family of asteroids, which are thought to have been formed by the breakup of a larger parent body.

Themis

Themis is a large asteroid that is about 130 miles (210 km) in diameter. It is known for its two large craters and its family of asteroids.

Cybele

Cybele is a large asteroid that is about 174 miles (280 km) in diameter. It is known for its long rotation period, which takes about 10.5 hours.

Hilda

Hilda is a group of asteroids that orbit the Sun in the 3:2 resonance with Jupiter. This means that they orbit the Sun three times for every two times that Jupiter orbits the Sun.
